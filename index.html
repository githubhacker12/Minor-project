Facts

The Taj Mahal was built with 27 different types of stones! Most people probably know that the Taj Mahal is constructed of marble, but did they know that it was built using 27 different types of stones that were carefully obtained from Tibet, China, Sri Lanka, and areas of India? Apart from that, material for the Taj Mahal's construction was brought in from nearly every corner of India and Asia.

START PLANNING YOUR TRIP TO HISTORICAL PLACES OF INDIA

So, you've decided to visit India? Great choice! Let's start planning your ultimate holiday.

Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal is one of the seven wonders of the world and is considered to be the most popular tourist attraction in the country. A symbol of love, the Taj was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for his queen Mumtaz. Tagore described the Taj as a "teardrop on the cheek of eternity". The monument attracts a lot of tourists all around the year.

  • Best Time to Visit : October to March

    Entry Fees : For Foreigners INR 1100 and for Indians INR 50

Qutub Minar, Delhi

At a height of 234 feet, this minaret is the tallest individual tower in the world and is a perfect display of Mughal architectural brilliance. The soaring and brave tower that allures tourists despite being destroyed by ravages of natural apocalypses several times, Qutub Minar is the tallest individual tower in the world and second tallest monument of Delhi.

  • Best Time to Visit : All year

    Entry Fees : INR 100 for Foreigners and NR 30 for Indians

Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

The Hawa Mahal stands at the intersection of the main road in Jaipur, Badi Chaupad. Built from red and pink sandstone, it is on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur, and women's chambers(Its 953 windows that cover the lace-like walls to allow royal ladies to watch the daily drama in the street below). It was built by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh.

  • Best Time to Visit : August to November

    Entry Fees : No Entry Fee Applicable

India Gate, Delhi

The All India War Memorial, popularly known as the India Gate, is a war memorial located in New Delhi. It is dedicated to the 82,000 soldiers, both Indian and British, who died during the First World War and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. The Amar Jawan Jyoti is the burning structure, right underneath the archway, immortal soldiers of India.

  • Best Time to Visit : October to March

    Entry Fees : For Foreigners INR 1100 and for Indians INR 50

Jallianwala Bagh, Amritsar

Jallianwala Bagh is a place of great historical importance during the Indian struggle for Independence. It was unoccupied and surrounded by a wall. It is a public garden in Amritsar which houses a memorial of national importance that was established by the government of India in 1951 to commemorate the massacre of peaceful celebrators by British forces on April 13,1919.

  • Best Time to Visit : October to March

    Entry Fees : For Foreigners INR 1100 and for Indians INR 50

Sun Temple, Konark

Dedicated to Lord Surya, this 13th-century temple is an architectural marvel. It is believed that the temple was constructed by King Narasimhadeva I of Eastern Ganga Dynasty. The shape of the temple is of a massive chariot with sumptuously engraved stone wheels, pillars and walls. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Best Time to Visit : October to March

    Entry Fees : For Foreigners INR 1100 and for Indians INR 50

Elephant Caves, Mumbai

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Elephanta Caves is a specimen of rock-cut art and architecture from the times of medieval India. This cave temple is dedicated (or) constructed to the Hindu god Shiva. It is an island and is located at a distance of 11km from the city of Mumbai. It also provides an amazing view of the Mumbai skyline.

  • Best Time to Visit : October to March

    Entry Fees : For Foreigners INR 1100 and for Indians INR 50

Orchha, Madhya Pradesh

Orchha is a town in Niwari district of Madhya Pradesh state, India. The town was established by rajput ruler Rudra Pratap Singh some time after 1501, as the seat of an eponymous former princely state of covering parts of central & north India, in the Bundelkhand region. Known for its grand palaces on banks of river Betwa Orchha.

  • Best Time to Visit : October to March

    Entry Fees : For Foreigners INR 1100 and for Indians INR 50
